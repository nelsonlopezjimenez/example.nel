Text-level elements

Text-level elements should be contained within block-level elements.  These elements affect the are generally used for emphasis or informational.  The two most common text-level elements are bold <strong> and italic <em>.  Both of these are container elements, they surround the text that they are making bold or italic.  Example the word important will be bold; the word important will be displayed in italic:

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html lang="en">
<head>
     <title>A Good Page Title Here</title>
     <meta charset="utf-8"/>
     <meta name="author" content="Marti Baker">
     <meta name="date" content="11/12/2013">
     <meta name="robots" content="NOINDEX, NOFOLLOW" />
</script>
</head>
<body>
<!-- Your visible page content -->
<p>This is <strong>important</strong> information.</p>

<p>This text is <em>italicized</em> for emphasis.</p>
</body>
</html>

     

    Hidden Comments

    Comments allow you to put notes to yourself or anyone else that may come behind you that needs to update a Web site.  Commenting you code not only is a good practice, consider it a Martism.  You will be required to comment things such as the start and stop of your navigation areas of your sites, your content areas, etc.  More to come later.  The basic syntax of a comment element is:

    <!DOCTYPE html>
    <html lang="en">
    <head>
         <title>A Good Page Title Here</title>
         <meta charset="utf-8"/>
         <meta name="author" content="Marti Baker">
         <meta name="date" content="11/12/2013">
         <meta name="robots" content="NOINDEX, NOFOLLOW" />
    </script>
    </head>
    <body>
    <!-- These are hidden comments.  Here is where you will add information that you want to have for later reference, but don't want it to be displayed to your viewing audience. -->
    </body>
    </html>
